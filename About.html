Tourism is the activity of traveling to and staying in places outside one’s usual environment for leisure, business, or other purposes, encompassing a wide range of experiences and services. Definition and Scope Tourism involves the movement of people for leisure, business, or other purposes, typically for more than 24 hours but less than one year, and includes both domestic and international travel. Tourists are individuals who engage in these activities, while excursionists or day-trippers visit destinations without overnight stays. Tourism is not just travel; it encompasses the services, activities, and infrastructure that create the overall visitor experience, including accommodations, food, attractions, and guided experiences. Wikipedia +2 History and Evolution Tourism has roots in ancient civilizations, such as Greece and Rome, where people visited historic sites, beaches, and the Seven Wonders of the World. Pilgrimages in Eastern civilizations, including early Buddhist sites and the Hajj to Mecca, also represent early forms of tourism. Modern tourism emerged in 17th-century Western Europe, evolving with organized travel, infrastructure, and leisure-focused experiences. Over time, technological advances like air travel, low-cost airlines, and online booking have made tourism more accessible and widespread.
